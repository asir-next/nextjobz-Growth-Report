Key observations — jobseeker
Top-of-funnel cooled after the Week 3 surge — expected normalisation
New visitors dipped −8% (7,605 → 6,994) and JS registrations pulled back −29.3% (2,436 → 1,723). This follows a classic post-spike correction after the sharp Week 3 jump. The numbers are still well above Week 2 levels (4,602 visitors, 1,432 registrations), so the platform base is holding.
CV completion has collapsed — the most alarming metric this week
Full CV builds (51–100%) crashed −73% (226 → 61). Partial builds also dropped −24.8% (2,210 → 1,662). The completion rate fell from an already-low 9.3% to just 3.5% of registrations. This is a three-week declining trend and needs an intervention — an automated email nudge, an in-app progress reminder, or a simpler CV builder flow.
Application activity hit an all-time high — jobseekers are the most engaged yet
Unique applicants grew +34.1% (1,906 → 2,556) and total jobs applied to rose +27% (411 → 522). Crucially, the average applications per applicant improved from 2.79 → 4.9 — meaning fewer new users registered this week, but those already on the platform are highly engaged and applying more actively than ever.
A platform split is forming — active users vs passive sign-ups
The data is telling two different stories simultaneously. Top-of-funnel (new visitors, registrations, CV builds) is contracting. But bottom-of-funnel (applications, jobs applied to) is accelerating. This suggests a maturing active user base is becoming more engaged, while new user acquisition and onboarding is starting to soften. Both need attention in different ways.

Key observations — employer
Employer registrations bounced back strongly — more than doubled
New company registrations surged +113.3% (15 → 32), the strongest single-week employer acquisition since tracking began. This directly recovers the sharp Week 3 drop and suggests the post-Eid employer hiring window is now fully open. The focus should be on converting these new registrants into active job posters quickly.
Job supply hit a new weekly high — 187 jobs published
Total published jobs grew +37.5% (136 → 187). This is the highest job volume since the platform launched. Combined with 2,556 unique applicants, the platform is increasingly resembling a functioning marketplace — more supply and more demand at the same time.
Paid listings continue to slide — now at an all-time low share
Paid jobs published fell again to just 8 (from 9 in W3, 12 in W2). The paid-to-total ratio is now at 4.3%, down from 11.9% in Week 2. Free listings dominate at 179 of 187 total jobs. As volume scales, this monetization gap widens. A targeted upsell prompt or featured listing incentive for new registrants this week would be timely.
Employer engagement with applications recovering — both metrics up
Unique companies viewing applications rose +12.5% (24 → 27) and total application views climbed +12.5% (104 → 117). After last week's sharp drop in views, this is a meaningful recovery. However, with 2,556 applicants submitting to 522 jobs, only 117 application views means the vast majority of applications are still going unseen. Improving employer notification and inbox UX remains the top priority.
