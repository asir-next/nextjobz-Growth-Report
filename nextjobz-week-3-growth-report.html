Key observations — jobseeker
Strong top-of-funnel momentum across all acquisition metrics
New visitors jumped +65% (4,602 → 7,605) and jobseeker registrations rose +70% (1,432 → 2,436). This is the most significant week-over-week acquisition growth the platform has seen, likely driven by the post-Eid hiring cycle picking up pace.
Partial CV builds surged — full completions did not follow
CV builds at the 0–50% tier exploded +85%, but completions at 51–100% actually fell −4.6%. The CV completion rate dropped from 16.5% → 9.3% of registered users. A large cohort of new users is signing up but not finishing their profiles — this is an onboarding gap that could be closed with a simple nudge or reminder.
Application activity surged — but average applications per applicant dipped slightly
Unique applicants rose +78.5% and total jobs applied to rose +33.4%. The average applications per unique applicant went from 3.46 (W2) → 2.79 (W3) — meaning more people are applying, but each person is applying to slightly fewer jobs. This suggests broader intent but shallower exploration.

Key observations — employer
Employer registrations dropped sharply — the most critical metric this week
New company registrations fell −42.3% (26 → 15). With jobseekers flooding the platform, a shrinking employer acquisition rate creates a growing supply-demand imbalance. This needs direct attention — targeted outreach to SMEs or a re-engagement campaign for employers who have not yet posted.
Existing employers are posting more — total published jobs up +35%
Despite fewer new registrations, total published jobs grew +34.7% (101 → 136). This means existing employers on the platform became more active in Week 3, likely responding to the post-Eid hiring window. A good sign — but growth cannot depend on existing employers alone.
Free listings growing fast — paid listings moving in the wrong direction
Free listings grew +42.7% (89 → 127) while paid listings dropped −25% (12 → 9). The paid-to-total ratio fell from 11.9% → 6.6%. As job volume scales, converting active free employers to paid tiers is an important monetization lever to activate.
Application view rate collapsed — employers are not reviewing enough
More companies viewed applications (+14.3%), but total application views dropped −34.6% (159 → 104). Given that applicants surged +78.5%, the ratio of application views to applications submitted is declining sharply. Employers may be overwhelmed or disengaged. Improving employer-side UX (smarter filtering, digest emails, alerts) could help here.
