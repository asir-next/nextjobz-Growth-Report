💡 Executive Insight — Jan to Mar 2026

January 2026 was the breakout month — the LinkedIn Recruiter launch on January 7 drove a 14× surge in new user registrations and pushed daily active users to a peak of 81,953. This was a defining inflection point in platform awareness.

February's cool-down is contextual, not structural. Three converging factors explain the dip: (1) the Bangladesh National Election disrupted mid-month activity, (2) February had 2–3 fewer calendar days than January, and (3) Ramadan onset shortened active working hours. New user acquisition still remained 2× above pre-LinkedIn baseline (Nov–Dec 2025).

March full-month data confirms stabilisation: 42,691 active users, 36,103 new users, 4,990 jobseeker registrations, and 342 jobs posted. Bounce rate improved to 26.9% — the best of any month in the 5-month window — suggesting improving content relevance. Focus for April: leverage post-Eid hiring surge and activate the referral + lead magnet pipeline.

📢 Google Ads Activity — March 2026

March marked the first significant Google Ads investment on the platform. Two campaigns ran during the month: "Website Visit March 26" (1,620 sessions) and "Eid-ul-Fitr AV Views till 22-3-26" (8 sessions). Combined with paid search (1,418 new users) and paid other (3,285 new users), this signals a deliberate shift toward paid acquisition diversification beyond the LinkedIn-organic model. This is a positive channel diversification trend to watch in April.
