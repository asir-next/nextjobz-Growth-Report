Key observations — jobseeker
Traffic decline continues — three consecutive weeks of falling visitors
New visitors fell −27.7% (3,385 → 2,446), now down −67.9% from the Week 3 peak of 7,605. Registrations held more steadily at −4% (1,437 → 1,379), meaning visitors who do arrive are more likely to sign up — but the top of funnel needs urgent attention before registration volume follows.
Full CV completions collapsed again — no stable trigger yet
CV builds (51–100%) dropped −67.4% (298 → 97), wiping out last week's recovery. The week-to-week volatility suggests there's no consistent mechanism driving completions. A persistent, always-on nudge — not a one-time push — is needed to stabilise this metric.
Application volume pulled back but remains the second-highest week on record
Unique applicants fell −30.4% (2,228 → 1,551) and jobs applied to dropped −37.2% (3,904 → 2,452). The correction from last week's spike was expected — 2,452 applications is still strong in context. The concern is whether the declining trend continues into next week.
Partial CV builds growing — funnel quality improving even as volume shrinks
CV builds (0–50%) grew +12.6% (1,139 → 1,282) despite fewer visitors and registrations. The funnel is becoming more efficient. The platform just needs more traffic at the top to translate this efficiency into real registration and completion volume.

Key observations — employer
Job supply collapsed — 41 published jobs is the lowest week on record
Total published jobs crashed −64.7% (116 → 41), the steepest single-week drop since the platform launched. Free listings drove the fall (112 → 32, −71.4%). With job supply this thin, applicant engagement will continue to fall. Immediate BD outreach to post new listings and re-activate dormant companies is the top priority.
Paid jobs hit a 4-week high — strongest monetisation signal yet
Paid job listings jumped +125% (4 → 9), the highest paid count since Week 2. The paid-to-total ratio recovered to 22% — up from the all-time low of 3.4% last week. Employers who posted this week chose to pay despite lower overall volume. This behaviour should be studied and the upsell prompt replicated at scale.
Employer engagement fell sharply — application views at a new low
Total application views dropped −61.2% (178 → 69) and unique companies viewing applications fell to just 17. With fewer jobs and fewer applicants, both sides of the marketplace contracted simultaneously. Employer re-engagement via inbox reminders and re-activation nudges is critical before this becomes a retention issue.
Company registrations held up — the pipeline is not broken
New company registrations grew +25% (16 → 20) despite the drop in job postings. Companies are still signing up — they're just not converting to active posters fast enough. Removing friction from the first job post remains the highest-leverage employer activation fix.
