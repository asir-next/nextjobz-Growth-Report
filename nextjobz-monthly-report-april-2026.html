Key observations — Jobseeker

Applicant activity exploded — platform demand is real now

Unique applicants jumped from 1,586 → 7,689 (+384.8%) and jobs with 10+ apps more than doubled (117 → 280). Jobs with 1–10 applicants also grew 164 → 226 (+37.8%) — demand is broad, not just concentrated in a few listings.

Fewer dead listings — job coverage is improving

Jobs with zero applicants fell 75 → 48 (−36%) even as the job pool grew 56%. Employers are seeing real candidate flow for the first time.

Engagement depth dipped — users apply and bounce

Avg. session dropped 2m 52s → 2m 16s (−20.9%) and bounce rate rose to 31.1%. Quick A/B test: show related jobs + a post-apply profile prompt to extend session depth.

Platform is entry-level heavy — senior talent gap is widening

86.3% of April registrants have 0–2 years of experience. But the top applied roles (Country Manager, Project Manager) are senior. Targeted outreach for mid-to-senior talent should be the next acquisition lever.

Key observations — Employer

Supply and demand grew together — marketplace is functioning

Company registrations +83.3% (48 → 88) and published jobs +56.3% (352 → 550). With 7,689 active applicants on the other side, April is the first month where both sides scaled in sync.

Dormant companies doubled — activation is the leak

26 companies registered but never posted — +136% vs March's 11. That's 29.6% of new sign-ups going cold. A simple "post your first job in 5 min" onboarding flow + 7-day nudge email is priority #1.

Shortlisting barely moved — applications are going unseen

Only 5 of 88 companies (5.7%) shortlisted any candidate against 7,689 applicants. Employer inbox UX and notification triggers need an overhaul before this becomes a retention issue for both sides.

Industry mix diversified — Garments & Education entered top 3

March was IT-dominant. April brought Education, Engineering & Construction and Garments/RMG (42 jobs, brand new). Broader industry coverage reduces concentration risk and opens new BD channels.
